# Miro Context & Positioning

This document contains Miro's current value propositions, key features, and strategic
positioning. Reference this when writing account research angles and outreach emails.

## Core Positioning

Miro is the visual workspace where teams collaborate to design, build, and ship
products — powered by AI. It's where strategy meets execution on a shared canvas.

## Primary Value Narratives

Use these as the foundation for outreach. Pick the one most relevant to the account
and the contact's role.

### 1. AI Transformation
Miro helps organizations bring AI into the way teams actually work — not as a
separate tool, but embedded in the collaborative workflows teams already use.
Key talking points:
- Miro AI assists directly on the canvas: brainstorming, clustering, summarizing,
  generating content
- MCP (Model Context Protocol) integration connects AI models to Miro workflows
- Teams can prototype AI-powered processes visually before building them
- Supports cross-functional AI adoption — not just engineering, but product, strategy,
  design, and operations

**Best for:** Director+ in Strategy, Innovation, Digital Transformation, Product

### 2. Product Acceleration
Miro accelerates the product development lifecycle from discovery to delivery.
Key talking points:
- Visual frameworks for user story mapping, journey mapping, sprint planning
- Real-time and async collaboration for distributed product teams
- Integrations with Jira, Confluence, Asana, and development tools
- Reduces meeting overhead — teams align visually instead of through status updates

**Best for:** VP/Director of Product, Engineering leads, Product Managers

### 3. Business Transformation & Strategic Planning
Miro provides the visual operating system for organizations undergoing change.
Key talking points:
- Enterprise-scale strategic planning on a shared canvas
- Process mapping and workflow optimization
- Change management visualization — making the invisible visible
- Cross-functional alignment across departments and geographies

**Best for:** Director+ in Strategy, Operations, Transformation, Program Management

### 4. Internal Productivity & Collaboration
Miro consolidates scattered collaboration tools into one visual workspace.
Key talking points:
- Replaces whiteboarding, sticky notes, slide decks, and multiple point solutions
- Works for meetings, workshops, brainstorms, retrospectives, and async work
- 200+ integrations with tools teams already use
- Reduces context-switching and tool sprawl

**Best for:** IT Admin, Director of IT, Operations, Department Heads, Managers

### 5. Enterprise Security & Admin
For IT and security-focused personas, lead with trust and control.
Key talking points:
- Enterprise Guard: advanced security, compliance, and data governance
- SSO, SCIM, domain management, content controls
- SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, HIPAA-eligible
- Admin analytics and usage reporting

**Best for:** IT Admin, CISO, Director of IT, Security leads

## Competitive Context

Don't trash competitors, but understand the landscape:
- **Lucidchart/Lucidspark**: More diagramming-focused, less collaborative workspace
- **FigJam (Figma)**: Design-team focused, less enterprise breadth
- **Microsoft Whiteboard**: Basic, lacks advanced features and AI
- **MURAL**: Similar space but Miro has broader adoption and stronger AI story

## Usage Signals That Matter

When you see these in the CSV data, they indicate engagement worth referencing:
- **High session count (50+)**: Power user — they know and like Miro
- **Recent activity (last 7 days)**: Currently active, warm outreach
- **MCP feature usage**: Forward-thinking, AI-interested
- **Multiple features used**: Expanding use case, ripe for conversation
- **Team of 5+ users at same account**: Organic growth happening

## The "Bottoms-Up" Story

Miro's XDR motion is bottoms-up: these contacts are already using Miro (often on free
or self-serve plans). The outreach is NOT cold — it's reaching out to people who
already experience value from Miro and exploring whether there's an opportunity to
expand that value across their organization with enterprise features, better security,
and AI capabilities.

Always frame outreach as: "I noticed your team is already using Miro — here's how
similar companies are getting even more out of it."

Never frame it as: "Would you like to try Miro?" — they're already using it.
