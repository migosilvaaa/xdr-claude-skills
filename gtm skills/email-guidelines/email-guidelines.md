# Email Guidelines for XDR Outreach

## Core Principles

These emails are being sent to people who **already use Miro**. This is not cold
outreach. It's a warm, bottoms-up motion. The tone should reflect that — you're
reaching out as a helpful resource, not a pushy salesperson.

### Tone
- Conversational, not corporate
- Confident but not aggressive
- Specific, not generic
- Curious, not assumptive
- Short — respect their time

### Structure

Every email follows this skeleton:

1. **Hook (1 sentence):** Something specific about their company or role that earns
   attention. Never start with "I hope this finds you well" or "My name is..."
2. **Bridge (1-2 sentences):** Connect the hook to Miro. Why are you reaching out
   to *this person* at *this company* right now?
3. **Value (1-2 sentences):** What Miro enables that's relevant to them. Be specific —
   reference a feature, a use case, or a result.
4. **CTA (1 sentence):** A soft question. Not "Can I get 15 minutes?" but something
   that invites a response.

### Length
- **Target: 80-120 words.** Shorter is better. If it scrolls on mobile, it's too long.
- **Subject line: 4-7 words.** Lowercase is fine. Specific beats clever.

## What Makes a Good Email

### Good Hooks (Specific, Earned Attention)
- "Saw {company} just announced {initiative} — that's a big undertaking."
- "Your team's been getting a lot of mileage out of Miro boards lately."
- "With {company} expanding into {area}, the cross-team coordination must be intense."

### Bad Hooks (Generic, Lazy)
- "I noticed you're in the {industry} space." (So are thousands of companies)
- "Are you looking to improve collaboration?" (Everyone says yes, no one responds)
- "I wanted to reach out because..." (They don't care why you wanted to)

### Good CTAs
- "Curious — is your team exploring how AI fits into your planning workflows?"
- "Would it be useful to see how {similar company} standardized on Miro for this?"
- "Happy to share what we're seeing work for {industry} teams — interested?"
- "Is expanding Miro usage across {department} something on your radar?"

### Bad CTAs
- "Can I get 15 minutes on your calendar?" (Too direct for first touch)
- "Let me know if you'd like a demo." (Premature)
- "Would love to connect!" (Empty)

## Persona-Specific Angles

### For Strategy / Transformation Leaders
Lead with: their company's strategic initiatives + how Miro supports that at scale
Reference: strategic planning, cross-functional alignment, change management, AI adoption
Avoid: tactical feature talk — they think in outcomes

### For Product Leaders
Lead with: product velocity, team alignment, reducing meeting overhead
Reference: roadmapping, user story mapping, sprint planning, Jira integration
Avoid: generic "collaboration" — be specific about product workflows

### For Engineering / Technical Leaders
Lead with: technical collaboration, architecture decisions, AI workflow integration
Reference: MCP, integrations, async collaboration, technical diagramming
Avoid: fluffy language — engineers appreciate directness and specifics

### For IT / Admin Personas
Lead with: tool consolidation, security, admin efficiency
Reference: Enterprise Guard, SSO/SCIM, usage analytics, compliance
Avoid: creative/collaboration messaging — they care about governance and cost

### For Managers / Team Leads
Lead with: team productivity, workshop facilitation, reducing tool sprawl
Reference: templates, async work, meeting efficiency
Avoid: enterprise-level messaging — keep it practical and team-focused

## Feature-Specific Email Angles

When the CSV shows specific feature usage, use it:

### MCP Users
These contacts are experimenting with AI workflows in Miro. They're forward-thinking.
Angle: "I saw your team's been exploring Miro's AI capabilities — we've been hearing
a lot of interest in this. Companies like {X} are using it to {specific use case}."

### High Session Count Users
These are power users who get real value from Miro.
Angle: "Your team is one of the most active Miro users at {company} — I'm curious
if there are capabilities on the enterprise side that could make that even smoother."

### Recent Feature Adopters
They're expanding how they use Miro — good signal.
Angle: "Noticed your team recently started using {feature} — that's exactly what
{similar company} was doing before they rolled Miro out across their {department}."

## Formatting Rules

- No bullet points in the email body (it's a personal email, not a newsletter)
- No bold or formatting — plain text only
- Use their first name, not "Hi there" or "Dear {full name}"
- Sign off with just the rep's first name (the sequence tool handles the full signature)
- One paragraph, maybe two. Never three.
- No links in first touch — earn the reply first

## Subject Line Patterns

Good patterns:
- `{company} + miro ai` — simple, relevant
- `quick question re: {initiative}` — curiosity-driven
- `{company}'s {department} + collaboration` — specific
- `saw {company}'s {news item}` — timely
- `miro usage at {company}` — direct

Avoid:
- ALL CAPS anything
- Emojis
- "Quick question" without context
- "Following up" on first touch
- Anything that sounds like marketing
